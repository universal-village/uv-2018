What to do

 

MIT Campus
  • Organized tours

Tour groups convene in Lobby 7, in the main building at 77 Massachusetts Avenue. Tours are offered at 11 am and 3 pm Monday-Friday (excluding legal holidays). Reservations are required for groups of 10 or more. Further questions about campus tours may be addressed to the Events and Information Center, 617-253-4795.

  • Self-guided tours

Maps to lead you on a self-guided tour of the MIT campus are available at the Events and Information Center, room 7-121 (see How to Read an MIT Address).

With its café, bookstores, and student community, Cambridge is often referred to as "Boston's Left Bank."

Famous squares in Cambridges

  • Central Square:  international restaurants and music clubs
  • Harvard Square: known for its bookstores and street performers
  • Inman Square: award-winning improv comedy
  • Kendall Square： home to MIT, the center of the high tech and biotechnology industries in Massachusetts
  • Porter Square： the region's largest concentration of Japanese eateries and shops in North Cambridge
  • Davis Square: a vibrant arts community in Somerville

 

Boston and Massachusetts

Before 1660, a trip to Boston from Cambridge involved a ferry ride and an eight-mile walk. You will find it more convenient to take the T or a cab across the river, or even walk. After all, it's only 364.4 smoots and one ear.

Hide out in the courtyard of the Boston Public Library, or see and be seen on Newbury Street. Take a Duck Tour in the city or a cruise through the Harbor Islands...Boston and Cambridge have something for everyone.

Shopping

Cambridge and Boston offer an eclectic mix of shopping emporia, from designer boutiques to vintage and thrift warehouses. Some of the major districts include:

  • CambridgeSide Galleria
  • Harvard Square
  • Faneuil Hall Marketplace
  • Garment District (actually, not a district, but an "alternative department store" with everything from feather boas to smoking jackets)
  • Newbury Street
  • Prudential Center
  • Copley Place
